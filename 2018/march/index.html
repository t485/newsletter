Point Reyes Reflections

Samuel Wong

Overall, the Point Reyes troop event was a success. The hike went smoothly even though all the scouts were tired. I thought I planned the hike well, but I could have improved by telling the length of the hike and the route ahead of time. The campout was also pretty good, but the campsites were much smaller than I expected. I guess I could have done more research on the size of the sites. Most people were really fatigued, so the cleanup process was slow and the cold really didn't help improve the situation. I could have delegated an backup SiC ahead of time because I had to leave early in the morning. As a result, the morning was less controlled and less organized than I expected because I just delegated an SiC on the spot.

Campout Attendance

Darren Yee

As you know, I am the new Troop scribe, and I want to use this space to talk about some of our attendance records. Our most recent campout was to Point Reyes. Only 23 scouts and parents went! Now, I understand that some people just came back from the snow retreat but other scouts may not have the same excuse. There weren’t that many people and we had to combine patrols to make up even half the amount of a normal patrol. I encourage everybody to try and show up to more campouts. Yes, they can be exhausting, but it’s worth it. You can get in some exercise and you can also gain more experience for another one. Please try and come.

Note from Jacob : I’ve noticed that campout attendances have decreased over the past three years even though the number of scouts registered has increased year by year. I want to let everybody know that these campouts aren’t “optional.” The choice of whether to go or not is yours, but please realize that many people work very hard to make these campouts happen. Our troop is more excellent when everybody shows up, and choosing not to show up does not add value to our troop.

Forest’s Eagle Project

Forest Yang

Hello! I'll be starting work on my eagle project soon, and I'll need your guys' help.

I (and a few other scouts from our troop) attend Monta Vista High School in Cupertino near HOC5. The school has a garden where students can plant, nurture, and harvest vegetables. However, in recent years, the construction of new school facilities has translocated the garden from one temporary spot to the next, resulting in its deterioration and a hiatus from gardening efforts. This year, the garden has finally received a permanent location, where it can start afresh in a newly cleared, large plot of land.

For my eagle project, I'll be building an automatic PVC pipe drip-irrigation system, which will provide a foundation for the garden to get back up and on its feet again. It'll make watering plants a lot easier and more environmentally-conscious, while helping the garden look neat and organized.

You can expect to hear from me within the next month regarding sign-ups for work shifts. I hope to see ya there =)
